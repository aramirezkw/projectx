CRM dashboard exploration

5 directions for new & underperforming agents

Each option targets a different failure mode: decision paralysis, invisible pipeline leakage, missing ambition, no felt momentum, or the blank-page problem. Patterns are sourced from Mobbin (Pipedrive, HubSpot, Attio, HoneyBook, ClickUp, Wrike, Todoist, ChatGPT, Gemini and more).

Daily Coach

For agents who freeze when they open the CRM

Warm greeting, a 4-stat strip, and one big "Today's 3 plays" card that picks itself — each play has a one-tap action. A streak chip and a setup checklist nudge progress without pressure.

Pipeline Health

Funnel chart with stage-to-stage conversion %. Worst stage is called out in plain English. Kanban below uses HubSpot-style "No activity · 14d" flags so cold leads can't hide.

Goal Ladder

Makes ambition concrete and stepwise

Big GCI ring, three sub-rings for the activities that drive it, a milestone ladder ("3 more appointments to hit your monthly target"), and a peer comparison band against agents at the same tenure.

Wins Feed

Makes small progress visible — builds confidence

Three columns: Do this week, In motion, Wins this week. Every reply, showing and signed offer becomes a card so the dashboard never feels dead — even for a slow week.

5
What do you want to work on?
Conversational AI-first

AI Copilot Home

Lowers the blank-page problem — just ask

Centered "What do you want to work on?" prompt with chips for the four jobs agents actually need help with: draft follow-ups, find leads to call, build a CMA, review pipeline. Below the fold: today's schedule and 3 leads to act on — so it's not all-or-nothing chat. Reuses the KWIQ shell, so this is the lightest implementation lift.

ChatGPT · Gemini · ClickUp Brain · KWIQ
Open prototype →
Recommendation — the strongest hybrid for this audience is 1 + 4 with a quiet 2: a coach-styled home (Option 1), a wins feed in the right rail (Option 4), and pipeline-health flags surfaced inline on lead cards (Option 2). Worth A/B testing Option 5 separately for agents already using KWIQ.
